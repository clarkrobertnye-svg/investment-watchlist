## FRAMEWORK: VALUE-CREATING vs VALUE-DESTROYING BUYBACKS

### âœ… VALUE-CREATING Buybacks
1. **Funded by FCF** - Not debt-financed
2. **Price < Intrinsic Value** - Buying at discount
3. **Share count actually decreases** - Not just offsetting dilution
4. **High ROIC company** - Limited better reinvestment opportunities
5. **Insiders holding** - Not selling while company buys

### âŒ VALUE-DESTROYING Buybacks  
1. **Funded by debt** - Leveraging up to buy back
2. **Buying at inflated prices** - Paying > intrinsic value
3. **Offsetting dilution only** - Share count flat or rising
4. **Low ROIC** - Should reinvest in business instead
5. **Insiders selling** - Dumping while company buys
6. **Cutting R&D/CapEx** - Sacrificing future for EPS games

---

## KEY METRIC: BUYBACK EFFECTIVENESS RATIO

```
Effective Buyback Ratio = % Share Count Reduction / Buyback Yield

> 0.8 = EFFICIENT (most buyback $ reducing shares)
0.5-0.8 = MODERATE (some dilution offset)
< 0.5 = POOR (mostly offsetting stock comp dilution)
```
